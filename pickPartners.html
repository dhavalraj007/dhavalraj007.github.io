Pick Partners

Lets say, We have 4 tennis players and want to form 2 doubles teams. How many ways can this be done?

It turns out there are 3 possible ways:

  • Team 1: (A, B) and Team 2: (C, D)
  • Team 1: (A, C) and Team 2: (B, D)
  • Team 1: (A, D) and Team 2: (B, C)

The general formula to calculate the number of ways to pair n players into doubles teams is:

n! / ((2!)^(n/2) * (n/2)!)

Here, n is the total number of players. For 4 players, we get:

4! / ((2!)^(4/2) * (4/2)!)
= 24 / (4 * 2)
= 3

So there are 3 ways to form 2 doubles teams from 4 players.

How this formula is derived

  • There are n! ways to arrange all n players (order matters and no repetitions).
  • We pair them into groups of 2. Since the order within each pair doesn't matter, divide by 2! for each of the n/2 pairs.
  • The order among the pairs themselves also doesn't matter, so divide by (n/2)!.

Another elegant way to think about it

Step-by-step pairing:

  • Start with the smallest-numbered player (say A). They can pair with any of the remaining n-1 players (B, C, or D). For example, pair with C.
  • Move to the next smallest player who hasn't been paired yet (say B). They can pair with any of the remaining n-3 players (D).
  • Repeat until all players are paired. The number of ways is:
(n-1) * (n-3) * (n-5) * ... * 1

This matches the formula above and feels more intuitive.

This formula above is also called double factorial some times( (n-1)!! ).
